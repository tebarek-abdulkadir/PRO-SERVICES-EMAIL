Dashboard Business Logic Spec
=============================

Purpose
-------
This document explains the business logic behind the main Dashboard views so a data analyst can understand what each metric means, how records are counted, and where interpretation can go wrong.

The focus here is business meaning, not technical implementation.


Executive Summary
-----------------
The Dashboard has 3 main business areas:

1. Chats
   Measures customer frustration and confusion.

2. Agents
   Measures agent response speed and working hours.

3. Sales
   Measures prospects, conversions, and revenue-related views.

The most important thing to know is that the word "conversion" does not mean the same thing everywhere in the dashboard.


==================================================
1. CHATS BUSINESS LOGIC
==================================================

What the Chats dashboard is trying to answer
--------------------------------------------
The Chats view is designed to answer:

- How many people showed frustration?
- How many people showed confusion?
- What issues are driving those outcomes?
- Are those rates getting better or worse over time?

What counts as a chat record
----------------------------
The chat logic works from already-classified conversation results.
Each record arrives with labels such as:

- frustrated or not
- confused or not
- main issues
- key phrases
- optional service/team context

The dashboard is not deciding frustration from scratch.
It is summarizing labels that were already assigned upstream.

How chat records are grouped
----------------------------
The business rule is to avoid overcounting the same person when they appear in multiple conversations.

The grouping priority is:

1. contract
2. client
3. maid
4. fallback conversation-level record

This means the dashboard tries to treat multiple chats from the same underlying person or household context as one person-level case for top-line metrics.

What the top-line chat metrics really mean
------------------------------------------
The most important rule:

- Chat percentages are person-based, not conversation-based.

So:

- Frustrated count = number of unique people with at least one frustrated interaction
- Confused count = number of unique people with at least one confused interaction
- Total = number of unique people reviewed that day

This matters because raw conversation volume can be much higher than the person count shown on the dashboard.

How the conversation list should be interpreted
-----------------------------------------------
The conversation list is a review surface for problem cases.
It is not the pure denominator for the headline percentages.

In other words:

- the list helps you inspect examples
- the top cards help you understand the size of the problem at a people level

How trends work
---------------
The trend view is effectively month-to-date.
It compares the selected day to earlier days in that same month rather than acting like a full long-term historical trend engine.

How issue insights work
-----------------------
Issue insights are driven by repeated issue themes found inside the frustrated or confused cases.

Business interpretation:

- "main issue" means the most commonly recurring issue among affected cases
- "top drivers" means the most frequent issues associated with frustration or confusion

What to be careful about
------------------------
1. A single person with multiple conversations should usually count once in the headline metrics.

2. The top cards and the problem-conversation list are not using the exact same counting concept.

3. The dashboard is only as good as the upstream classification.
   If frustration or confusion labeling is noisy, the dashboard will reflect that.

4. Cumulative trend views may feel larger than daily unique-person views because the same person can appear on more than one day.


==================================================
2. AGENTS BUSINESS LOGIC
==================================================

What the Agents dashboard is trying to answer
---------------------------------------------
The Agents view is designed to answer:

- How fast are agents responding?
- Who are the fastest and slowest responders?
- How many hours did agents work?
- Is response performance lining up with staffing effort?

Two business inputs feed this view
----------------------------------
The dashboard combines two separate business feeds:

1. Response-time feed
   The speed at which agents reply.

2. Hours-worked feed
   Agent login/logout and total hours logged.

These two feeds are related but independent.

What the main metrics mean
--------------------------
- Active agents = agents included in the response-time feed for that day
- Daily average response time = overall benchmark for the day
- Agent ranking = ordered by fastest average response time
- Total hours logged = total staffing effort recorded that day
- Average hours per agent = average effort level across agents

How the ranking works
---------------------
Lower response time is better.

So:

- faster response = higher performance rank
- slower response = lower performance rank

How hours are combined with response time
-----------------------------------------
Hours are used as supporting context, not as the ranking metric itself.

The dashboard is essentially asking:

- who responded quickly,
- and how many hours did that person work?

Business interpretation:

- a fast response time with low hours may suggest high efficiency or a small shift
- a slow response time with high hours may suggest workload or process issues

What to be careful about
------------------------
1. Response time is the primary metric.
   Hours do not change ranking order.

2. Hours data may be missing for some dates or some agents.
   The response-time view can still exist without a complete hours picture.

3. If names are inconsistent between sources, some hours may not attach cleanly to the correct agent.

4. Date selection is mainly day-based for business interpretation, even if the interface visually suggests more flexibility.


==================================================
3. SALES BUSINESS LOGIC
==================================================

What the Sales area is trying to answer
---------------------------------------
The Sales side of the dashboard is designed to answer:

- How many prospects were identified?
- How many of those prospects converted?
- Which services are strongest?
- How do counts break down by contract type or country?
- What do revenue and profit look like over time?

The biggest source of confusion
-------------------------------
There are multiple business definitions of conversion in this system.

They should not be treated as interchangeable.

There are 4 main commercial views:

1. Prospect volume
2. Complaint-based conversion
3. Payment-based conversion
4. P&L / revenue logic

There is also a separate overseas-sales measure from To Do records.


3A. Prospect logic
------------------

What a prospect is
------------------
A prospect is a conversation or household that was identified as having interest in one of the tracked services, such as:

- OEC
- OWWA
- Travel Visa
- Filipina Passport Renewal
- Ethiopian Passport Renewal

How prospects are counted
-------------------------
Prospects are not always counted as simple raw rows.
The business logic tries to reduce double counting by grouping related records together, especially at household or contract level.

This means:

- multiple related records can collapse into one prospect opportunity
- counts are intended to reflect real opportunities more than raw chat volume

Why prospect counts can change after filtering
----------------------------------------------
Before the main dashboard reports prospect counts, it removes prospects that already had earlier complaints before the selected day.

Business meaning:

- the dashboard is trying to focus on fresh eligible opportunities
- not every historically identified prospect remains in scope for the selected day

What contract type and country breakdowns mean
----------------------------------------------
- Contract type breakdown shows how prospect opportunities split across major contract categories
- Country breakdown applies mainly to travel visa prospects

These breakdowns are best interpreted as segmented opportunity counts, not raw inquiry counts.


3B. Complaint-based conversion logic
------------------------------------

What it means
-------------
In the main Sales dashboard, conversion usually means:

- a prospect that also has a matching complaint outcome on the selected day

This is the core business rule behind the main conversion cards in the daily sales view.

Business interpretation:

- the system is using complaint activity as evidence that the prospect moved into a converted state for that service

Why this matters
----------------
This is not the same thing as payment collection.

So if someone asks:

"Why do dashboard conversions not equal cash received?"

The answer is:

- because the main dashboard is not using payment as its primary conversion definition

When to use this metric
-----------------------
Use complaint-based conversion when you want to understand operationally recognized service completions or downstream complaint-linked outcomes tied to prospects.


3C. Payment-based conversion logic
----------------------------------

What it means
-------------
There is a separate conversion view that uses payments.

In that view, conversion means:

- a prospect with a received payment on the selected day

Business interpretation:

- this is closer to a commercial or cash-confirmed conversion view

Why it can disagree with the main Sales dashboard
-------------------------------------------------
Payment-based conversion and complaint-based conversion are built from different business events.

So they can differ because:

- some cases have complaint evidence but no same-day payment
- some cases have payment evidence but do not appear as complaint-linked conversions
- timing can differ across systems

How to use it
-------------
Use payment-based conversion when you want a stricter financial conversion lens.


3D. Clean conversion logic
--------------------------

What it means
-------------
The system also supports a "clean conversion" view.

This asks:

- of the prospects that converted by payment, how many did so without relevant complaint issues?

Business interpretation:

- overall conversion rate tells you volume converted
- clean conversion rate tells you volume converted without complaint signals

This is useful for quality-adjusted conversion analysis.


3E. P&L logic
-------------

What the P&L view is trying to answer
-------------------------------------
The P&L view is designed to answer:

- what service volume happened in a period
- what revenue that volume implies
- what cost that volume implies
- what gross profit and net profit look like

What drives the P&L volume
--------------------------
The default logic is complaint-driven, not payment-driven.

Business meaning:

- complaint activity is treated as the primary signal for service volume
- that volume is then translated into revenue and cost using pricing rules

How duplicate sales are handled
-------------------------------
The P&L logic applies a 3-month deduplication rule.

Business rule:

- repeated complaint activity for the same service/person combination within 3 months is treated as the same sale
- if it happens more than 3 months later, it can count as a new sale

Why pricing can change across time
----------------------------------
The P&L logic uses different pricing before and after a specific price-change date.

Business interpretation:

- the same service may produce different revenue or margin depending on when the sale happened

What to be careful about
------------------------
1. P&L is not a direct payment ledger.
   It is primarily a modeled financial view based on complaint-derived service volume.

2. Revenue trends can move because of both:
   - volume changes
   - pricing changes

3. If complaint capture is incomplete, P&L volume may understate business reality.


3F. Overseas sales from To Dos
------------------------------

What it means
-------------
There is also a separate sales-like measure built from overseas-related To Do records.

Business rule:

- multiple To Dos close together for the same underlying case are treated as one sale period
- if they are far enough apart, they can count as more than one sale

How to use it
-------------
This should be treated as a separate operational sales proxy.

It should not automatically be added into:

- complaint-based conversions
- payment-based conversions
- P&L conversion counts

unless the business team explicitly decides to unify them.


==================================================
4. KEY DEFINITIONS
==================================================

Prospect
--------
A person, household, or case identified as having interest in one of the tracked services.

Main dashboard conversion
-------------------------
A prospect that is treated as converted based on same-day complaint-linked logic.

Payment conversion
------------------
A prospect treated as converted because a received payment exists on the selected day.

Clean conversion
----------------
A payment conversion without relevant complaint signals.

P&L volume
----------
A deduplicated count of service activity used to estimate revenue and profit.

Chat total
----------
A unique-person count, not a raw conversation count.


==================================================
5. MOST IMPORTANT BUSINESS WARNINGS
==================================================

1. Never assume "conversion" means one single thing across the dashboard.

2. The main Sales dashboard conversion is not the same as payment conversion.

3. Prospect counts are filtered.
   A previously flagged prospect may be excluded from a later day if prior complaint history exists.

4. Chats are person-based at the top level.
   Do not compare chat totals directly to raw conversation counts.

5. Agents are judged primarily on response speed, with hours used as supporting context.

6. P&L is modeled from service activity signals, not simply from payments received.

7. Overseas To Do sales are a separate business view and should not be mixed into other conversion logic without explicit alignment.


==================================================
6. HOW TO TALK ABOUT THE DASHBOARD
==================================================

If presenting to business stakeholders, the safest language is:

- Chats shows customer experience risk at a people level.
- Agents shows service responsiveness and staffing context.
- Sales shows opportunity volume, but conversion must be clarified by method.
- Main dashboard conversions are operational/complaint-linked.
- Payment conversions are commercial cash-linked.
- P&L is a modeled profitability view based on service activity.


==================================================
7. SIMPLE REFERENCE
==================================================

Use this cheat sheet:

- If you want customer pain: use Chats.
- If you want service speed: use Agents.
- If you want fresh opportunity counts: use main Sales prospects.
- If you want operational conversion: use main Sales conversions.
- If you want payment-confirmed conversion: use payment conversion logic.
- If you want quality-adjusted conversion: use clean conversion logic.
- If you want modeled profitability: use P&L.


End of business spec.
